The zig-zag is necessary because equivalent derivations need not produce interpolant formulae that are connected by a simple chain of derivations. In particular, there are equivalent derivations whose interpolants do not imply each other, but can imply or be implied by the interpolant extracted from a third equivalent derivation. For example, consider these two equivalent derivations, ⊗R (⊗R (⇒L (f₀ , f'₀) , f''₀), ⇒L (f₁ , (⊗R (f'₁ , f''₁)))) ≗ ⊗R (⇒L (f₀ , ⊗R (f'₀ , f''₀)) , ⊗R (⇒L (f₁ , f'₁) , f''₁)). The two interpolant formulae are ((D₀ ⇒ E₀) ⊗ F₀) ⊗ (D₁ ⇒ (E₁ ⊗ F₁)) and (D₀ ⇒ (E₀ ⊗ F₀)) ⊗ ((D₁ ⇒ E₁) ⊗ F₁), respectively. They do not imply each other, however, they are both implied by ((D₀ ⇒ E₀) ⊗ F₀) ⊗ ((D₁ ⇒ E₁) ⊗ F₁), the interpolant formula of the derivation ⊗R (⇒L (f₀ , ⊗R (f'₀ , f''₀)) , ⇒L (f₁ , (⊗R (f'₁ , f''₁)))), which is equivalent to the two derivations above.

We study proof-relevant interpolation since we are interested in categorical soundness and completeness, which leads us to consider proof equivalences that are related to morphism equalities in the corresponding categorical models. Maehara's method is usually used to provide an interpolant formula for sequent calculi. However, its construction also records information about derivations, and for many logics, including classical and intuitionistic (linear) logics, it has been shown that the derivations extracted from Maehara's method are equivalent to the original derivation after cut (composition). The interpolation triples can thus be thought of as factorizations of morphisms in the corresponding categorical models. Well-definedness is a step further, where we study the structure of interpolation triples (factorizations). We also realized, after submission, that our notion of equivalence of interpolation triples is close in spirit to Pavlović's paper "Categorical interpolation: Descent and the Beck-Chevalley condition without direct images", but in a multicategorical setting. We had not made this connection explicit in the submitted version and we will add the reference. In a multicategory, a morphism may have a list of objects as domain, so it has a more natural link to sequent calculi. Proof-relevant interpolation and well-definedness for the product-free Lambek calculus, on the other hand, would correspond to the study of the interpolation condition on non-representable closed multicategories. Given the strong connection between sequent calculi and multicategories, we believe that our work can serve as a first step toward studying interpolation for multicategories.

As for relevance to logic and automated reasoning, one reason we find well-definedness important is that interpolation is often understood as an extraction procedure from proofs. A well-defined interpolation procedure could lead to an equational theory of interpolants, where one only has to extract an interpolant once, and the interpolants of equivalent derivations can then be obtained from it by composition.
