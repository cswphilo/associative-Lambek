We agree with the reviewers that the paper currently does not include an extended reflection, or bird-eye view, on the connection between the structure of Maehara interpolants, cut admissibility and equality of proofs in substructural logics. But we believe that such a perspective can be given using the language of category theory, in particular employing the multicategories of Lambek. Multicategory have morphisms with a list of objects as domain, not necessarily a single object, and as such have strong connections to sequent calculi for variants of intuitionistic logic.

In multicategorical models of sequent calculus, the cut rule is intepreted as morphism composition and interpolant triples would be interpreted as certain factorizations of morphisms. In this setting, studying well-definedness and equivalence of interpolation triples translates to understanding the structure of such factorizations. After submission, we realized that this perspective is similar to the one in PavloviД‡'s paper "Categorical interpolation: Descent and the Beck-Chevalley condition without direct images", but for multicategories instead of categories.

We plan to undertake this multicategorical investigation of interpolation in future work, but we strongly believe that the results described in our paper, which are of syntactic, proof-theoretic nature, will be crucial for unraveling the more general and illuminating categorical story. We will add a paragraph on this perspective in the introductory section, where we will also describe the relationship of our work with the other (few) attempts at a category-theoretic understanding of Craig interpolation (PavloviД‡, ДЊubriД‡, Pitts, Makkai).

* R2: "why the technical developments in the paper are of particular interest to the logic and automated reasoning community"

As for relevance to logic and automated reasoning, one reason we find well-definedness important is that interpolation is often understood as an extraction procedure from proofs. A well-defined interpolation procedure could lead to an equational theory of interpolants, where one only has to extract an interpolant once, and the interpolants of equivalent derivations can then be obtained from it by composition.
